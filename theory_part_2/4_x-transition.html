Example: Usage of x-transition + Tailwind Classes

In this example we give a more detailed description how the transition should work, especially differences between fading-in and fading-out.

Example

  1. x-transition:enter contains the properties of "fading-in" = Appearance
    x-transition:leave contains the properties of "fading-out" = Disappearance
  2. In "..-start" you place CLASSES with properties at the beginning of the fading-in or fading-out.
  3. In "..-end" you place CLASSES with properties at the beginning of the fading-in or fading-out.
  4. Example:
    x-transition:enter="transition duration-4000"
    x-transition:enter-start="opacity-0"
    x-transition:enter-end="opacity-100"
    x-transition:leave="transition duration-1000"
    x-transition:leave-start="opacity-100"
    x-transition:leave-end="opacity-0"

Own transition classes

  1. You can use your own transition classes, or combine the one's of TailwindCss with your own.
  2. For example, if you want to have a transition length of 4 seconds, which does not exist in Tailwind, you can add your own class:
    .duration-4000 { transition-duration: 4s; }
